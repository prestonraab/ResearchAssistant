### ⚠️ C_72: Walter et al. [2016] - REQUIRES REVISION
**Status**: CLAIM TECHNICALLY CORRECT BUT MISALIGNED WITH MANUSCRIPT USAGE

**Current Claim**: "The Walter et al. [2016] study investigated adaptation of Mycobacterium tuberculosis to impaired host immunity in HIV-infected patients"

**Primary Quote**: Title: "Adaptation of Mycobacterium tuberculosis to Impaired Host Immunity in HIV-Infected Patients"

**The Problem**: 
1. The claim header is technically correct - the study DID investigate M. tuberculosis adaptation to HIV-associated immune impairment
2. HOWEVER, the manuscript (line 205, 215, 218) uses this study as if it's part of a collection examining "active vs latent TB" classification
3. The Walter2016 study actually compares **HIV+ vs HIV- patients who ALL have active TB** - it's a completely different classification task
4. From the paper: "We compared M. tuberculosis and human gene transcription in sputum between HIV-infected and uninfected patients with tuberculosis"

**How it's used in manuscript.md**:
- Line 205: Listed alongside studies examining "TB progression risk and HIV-associated differences in TB pathogenesis"
- Line 215: Table entry: "HIV+ vs HIV- in active TB patients (different classification task)"
- Line 218: Grouped with other studies as if they all address similar classification tasks

**The manuscript DOES acknowledge** it's a "different classification task" in the table, but the claim doesn't make this clear.

**Recommended Actions**:

**Option 1 - Revise the claim header to be more specific:**
```markdown
## C_72: The Walter et al. [2016] study compared M. tuberculosis gene expression between HIV-infected and HIV-uninfected patients with active tuberculosis in Gambia and Uganda
```

**Option 2 - Add clarification to existing claim:**
Add after the current claim header:
```markdown
**Note**: This study examines HIV status in patients with active TB (HIV+ vs HIV-), not active vs latent TB classification like most other studies in this collection.
```

**Option 3 - Revise manuscript usage:**
Consider whether Walter2016 should be included in the dataset collection at all, since it addresses a fundamentally different biological question (M. tuberculosis adaptation to HIV) rather than TB diagnosis/progression.
